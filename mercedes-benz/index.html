Background

Why Tolerance Analysis Matters

In mass-produced lithium-ion battery packs, no two cells are identical. Manufacturing spreads, sensor biases, and software estimation errors all compound — impacting charging time, discharge power, and long-term ageing differently for each customer.

Thesis Focus

Quantifying HW · EE · SW Tolerance Effects

Investigated how hardware (cell manufacturing spread), electrical (sensor errors), and software (BMS estimation) tolerances interact to shape key performance indicators (charging time, discharge power, energy capacity) and battery ageing over lifetime — from BOL to EOL.

Virtual Model

Thermo-Electric Battery Model in MATLAB/Simulink

A two-cell thermo-electric model representing the full 192-cell Li-NMC pack in series configuration — validated to capture electrochemical and thermal dynamics with high fidelity.

Model Architecture

ECM + Thermal Coupling

Electrical: Equivalent Circuit Model (ECM) with single R-C Pair Thevenin representation captures terminal voltage as function of OCV(SOC,T), impedance Z_eq(SOC,T), and current. Thermal: Bernardi model computes heat generation from ohmic and entropic sources; coolant heat exchange models realistic thermal propagation.

Terminal Voltage Equation:

v_bat(t) = v_OCV(SOC,T) + Z_eq(SOC,T) · i_bat(t)

Tolerance Landscape

HW · EE · SW Sources

Tolerances span three domains — each affecting different system layers and interacting through the BMS control loop.

⬡ Hardware (HW)

Cell Manufacturing

Capacity spread, internal resistance variation, OCV scatter, coolant temperature variance.

⬡ Electrical (EE)

Sensor Errors

Voltage (V), current (I), temperature (T) sensor biases and noise — feed into BMS estimation.

⬡ Software (SW)

BMS Algorithms

SOC estimation error, capacity estimation error, passive cell balancing inaccuracies.

Analysis Methods

Three-Layer Sensitivity Study

Applied sequentially from deterministic sweeps to full probabilistic global analysis.

① LSA — Morris EE

Local Sensitivity

Parameter sweeps within tolerance bands; Elementary Effects method ranks parameters by influence on KPIs without full probabilistic sampling.

② MC-OAT

One-at-a-Time Monte Carlo

Normal distributions applied to one parameter at a time; output KPI distributions analysed via mean shift and spread — accounts for probability of occurrence.

③ GSA — Monte Carlo

Global Sensitivity

All tolerances simultaneously sampled. Captures real-world interactions and compounding effects. KPI distributions compared at BOL vs. EOL.

KPIs Evaluated

Performance Metrics at BOL & EOL

Evaluated under customer-relevant operating conditions at beginning of life (BOL) and end of life (EOL).

  • • Total charging time

⬇ Discharging — 30 s peak demand cycle
